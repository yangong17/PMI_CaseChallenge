Scenario 1: Fire Marshal Adds Equipment Requirement

Your team is midway through execution when the unexpected happens. During the facility inspection, the fire marshal informs you that a Double Check Detector Assembly (DCDA) is now required for backflow prevention. This component is essential to obtain final approval for your sprinkler system - but it was not included in the original scope or budget due to it being a new permit requirement.

The DCDA will:

  • Add approximately $15,000 to your project cost (equipment, labor, and re-inspection)
  • Take up to 30 days to order and install, potentially delaying downstream tasks

Objective

A completed Change Request Form including:

  • A brief description of the issue and trigger event
  • An analysis of scope, schedule, and cost impact
  • Options considered (e.g., deferral, value engineering, vendor negotiation)
  • Your team's recommended action

Change Request Hints (Discuss as a Team)

  • Could the requirement be met through a different type of fire suppression system or engineering workaround?
  • What options might reduce the delay or cost, even if they involve trade-offs?

Your team is preparing for the paint booth installation when you receive an unexpected update from your vendor. Due to logistics and supply chain issues, the originally ordered booth model will be delayed by an additional 30 days, beyond the original 30-day estimate. This delay puts pressure on your project timeline and risks pushing downstream activities into conflict with permit deadlines and resource availability.

The vendor offers an alternative:

  • A different booth model with similar specifications
  • Available in 21 days â€” 9 days earlier than the original unit
  • $10,000 more expensive
  • Slightly more energy-efficient, which could yield long-term savings

Your team must now evaluate whether to accept the delay or proceed with the alternative. This scenario requires careful analysis of cost vs. time trade-offs, potential operational benefits, and how the decision affects the overall project baseline.

Objective

A completed Change Request Form including:

  • A brief description of the issue and trigger event
  • An analysis of scope, schedule, and cost impact
  • Options considered (e.g., deferral, value engineering, vendor negotiation)
  • Your team's recommended action

Change Request Hints (Discuss as a Team)

  • What are the long-term implications of selecting the more energy-efficient model?
  • Is it worth paying more to accelerate the timeline?
  • Could other activities be re-sequenced to absorb the 30-day delay instead?
  • Are there ways to negotiate with the vendor on cost or delivery?

Scenario 3: AQMD Permit Hold â€“ Additional Drawings Required

As your project progresses through permitting, the Air Quality Management District (AQMD) places your permit on hold. They request detailed building plans that clearly show the location of the paint booth's exhaust to verify that it is at least 500 feet away from any residential or school property - a condition not previously flagged in the initial review.

To fulfill this requirement, your team must:

  • Hire a contractor or civil engineer to produce compliant drawings
  • Submit the drawings to AQMD for review and approval

This unexpected step introduces a delay and adds costs for design and coordination services. Your team must decide how best to respond while maintaining progress on the rest of the project and managing stakeholder expectations.

  • Estimated cost impact: +$2,000
  • Schedule impact: +14 days (drawing, submission, and review)

Objective

A completed Change Request Form including:

  • A brief description of the issue and trigger event
  • An analysis of scope, schedule, and cost impact
  • Options considered (e.g., alternate placement, expediting review, temporary deferral)
  • Your team's recommended action

Change Request Hints (Discuss as a Team)

  • What can be done in parallel to reduce the impact of the 14-day delay?
  • Is there a way to expedite AQMD review through additional coordination or fees?
  • Should the team update the risk register to account for future regulatory requests?
