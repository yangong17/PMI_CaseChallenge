2.4.1. Mapping the Risks

Scenario Continuation

Now that your schedule and cost baseline are established, it's time to evaluate risks - events that could delay or disrupt the project. Identifying risks early allows the team to plan appropriate responses and minimize impact.

Review memos from stakeholders outlining potential project risks, and develop a Risk Matrix to prioritize them based on likelihood and impact.

A Risk Matrix is a visual tool used in project management to evaluate and prioritize risks based on two factors: the likelihood of the risk occurring and the impact it would have on the project. It helps teams identify which risks need immediate attention and which can be monitored over time.
