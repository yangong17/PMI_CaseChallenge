1. Initiation

Overview:

  • The initiation phase is where all projects begin
  • It establishes the purpose and scope of the project and obtains necessary approvals
  • Where project viability is confirmed

There are two main processes within the Initiation phase according to the PMBOK Guide:

  1. Develop Project Charter: This is the process of developing a document that formally authorizes the existence of a project and provides the project management with the authority to apply organizational resources to project activities. The charter includes information such as project purpose, objectives, scope, key stakeholders, and the members of the project management team.

  2. Identify Stakeholders: This process involves identifying all people or organizations affected by the project, and documenting relevant information regarding their interests, involvement, and impact on project success. This is crucial for ensuring that the needs and expectations of stakeholders are considered throughout the project.
